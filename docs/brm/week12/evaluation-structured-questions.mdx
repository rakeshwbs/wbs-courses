### **Sample Examination Questions & Answers - Week 12 Concepts**

**QUESTION 1 [25 MARKS]**

(a) Define the three major types of research misconduct: **Plagiarism, Fabrication, and Falsification**.
[12]

<details>
<summary>Click to see the answer and marking scheme</summary>

**Answer:**

1. **Plagiarism:** Presenting someone else's work, ideas, or words as your own without giving them proper credit or citation.
2. **Fabrication:** Inventing or making up data or results and then recording or reporting them as if they were real.
3. **Falsification:** Manipulating research materials, equipment, or processes, or changing or omitting data or results, so the research is not accurately represented in the record.

**Marking Scheme:**
* [4 Marks] - Correct definition of Plagiarism.
* [4 Marks] - Correct definition of Fabrication.
* [4 Marks] - Correct definition of Falsification.

</details>

(b) A junior researcher is excited about a study that supports their hypothesis. Two outlier data points contradict the findings, so they delete them without mentioning it in the report.

1. What specific type of research misconduct is this?
2. Explain the ethical principle of **Informed Consent**.
3. Explain the difference between **Confidentiality** and **Anonymity**.
[13]

<details>
<summary>Click to see the answer and marking scheme</summary>

**Answer:**

1. **Misconduct:** **Falsification**, because data was improperly omitted to make the results look better.
2. **Informed Consent:** Requires providing potential participants with complete information about the study’s purpose, procedures, risks, and benefits, allowing them to voluntarily agree to participate, with the option to withdraw at any time.
3. **Confidentiality vs. Anonymity:**
   * **Confidentiality** – The researcher knows participants’ identities but ensures they will not be linked to their data in any public report.
   * **Anonymity** – The researcher does not know the identity of the participants at all.

**Marking Scheme:**
* [3 Marks] - Correctly identifying misconduct as Falsification.
* [5 Marks] - Clear definition of Informed Consent.
* [5 Marks] - Clear differentiation between Confidentiality and Anonymity.

</details>

**QUESTION 2 [25 MARKS]**

(a) Describe the **peer-review process** in academic publishing. What is its main purpose?
[12]

<details>
<summary>Click to see the answer and marking scheme</summary>

**Answer:**

The **peer-review process** assesses the quality of a manuscript before publication. Once submitted, the journal editor sends it to experts in the same field, who evaluate its methodology, analysis, originality, and significance. They then recommend acceptance, revision, or rejection.

**Main Purpose:** Acts as a **quality control** mechanism, ensuring published research is valid, credible, original, and valuable to the field.

**Marking Scheme:**
* [8 Marks] - Clear description of the process (submission, expert review, decision).
* [4 Marks] - Main purpose stated as quality control for scientific research.

</details>

(b) Imagine you are preparing a manuscript for submission to a journal.

1. What is a journal's **Impact Factor**, and what does it generally signify?
2. What is the purpose of the **Acknowledgements** section?
3. What is the purpose of a **Copyright Waiver or Transfer Agreement**?
[13]

<details>
<summary>Click to see the answer and marking scheme</summary>

**Answer:**

1. **Impact Factor:** Metric showing the average number of citations received by articles in the journal over a set period. It is often seen as an indicator of prestige and influence in the field.
2. **Acknowledgements:** Thanks individuals or organizations who contributed support but do not meet authorship criteria (e.g., funding, advice, access to facilities).
3. **Copyright Waiver/Transfer:** Legal agreement transferring exclusive rights to the publisher, allowing them to reproduce, publish, and distribute the article.

**Marking Scheme:**
* [5 Marks] - Correct definition of Impact Factor and significance.
* [4 Marks] - Purpose of the Acknowledgements section.
* [4 Marks] - Purpose of a Copyright Waiver.

</details>
