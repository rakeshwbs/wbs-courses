### **Concept: Sample Standard Deviation from a Frequency Table**

The **standard deviation** is a measure of how spread out the numbers in a dataset are from their mean (average). A low standard deviation indicates that the data points are clustered closely around the mean, while a high standard deviation indicates they are spread out over a wider range.

The formula you provided is used to calculate the **sample standard deviation (s)** for ungrouped data that is presented in a frequency table.


**Breaking Down the Formula:**
* **s**: The symbol for the sample standard deviation.
* **Σ**: The Greek letter Sigma, which means "the sum of".
* **f**: The frequency of each data value (how many times it appears).
* **x**: The actual data value.
* **Σf**: The sum of all the frequencies, which is the total number of data points in the sample (`n`).
* **Σfx**: The sum of each data value multiplied by its frequency.
* **Σfx²**: The sum of each data value **squared**, then multiplied by its frequency.

To use this formula, you expand the frequency table with columns to calculate the necessary totals.

### **Worked Examples from Past Papers**

:::danger 3 September 2019, Section B, Question 24
The number of times a group of people go shopping each week is shown in the table below. Calculate the sample standard deviation of the data.

| Number of times people go shopping | Frequency |
| :--- | :---: |
| 1 | 21 |
| 2 | 27 |
| 3 | 19 |
| 4 | 15 |
| 5 | 11 |
| 6 | 5 |
| 7 | 2 |
:::
:::success Answer
Standard Deviation ≈ 1.71 ✔️
:::note Explanation

1.  **Create a working table:**

| Value (x) | Frequency (f) | f × x | x² | f × x² |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 1 | 21 | 21 | 1 | 21 |
| 2 | 27 | 54 | 4 | 108 |
| 3 | 19 | 57 | 9 | 171 |
| 4 | 15 | 60 | 16 | 240 |
| 5 | 11 | 55 | 25 | 275 |
| 6 | 5 | 30 | 36 | 180 |
| 7 | 2 | 14 | 49 | 98 |
| **Total**| **Σf = 100** | **Σ(fx) = 291** | | **Σ(fx²) = 1135**|
2.  **Substitute totals into the formula:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{(\sum f) - 1} \left[ \sum fx^2 - \frac{(\sum fx)^2}{(\sum f)} \right]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{100 - 1} \left[ 1135 - \frac{(291)^2}{100} \right]}$$
3.  **Calculate:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{99} \left[ 1135 - \frac{84681}{100} \right]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{99} [1135 - 846.81]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{99} [288.19]} \approx \sqrt{2.911} \approx 1.706$$
:::

:::danger 1 March 2016, Section B, Question 21
A survey asks 75 people the number of times they shop in the town centre each week. Calculate the standard deviation. Give your answer to 3 significant figures.

| Number of times | Frequency |
| :--- | :---: |
| 0 | 6 |
| 1 | 18 |
| 2 | 20 |
| 3 | 12 |
| 4 | 9 |
| 5 | 7 |
| 6 | 3 |
:::
:::success Answer
Standard Deviation ≈ 1.62 ✔️
:::note Explanation

1.  **Create a working table:**

| Value (x) | Frequency (f) | f × x | x² | f × x² |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 0 | 6 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 1 | 18 | 18 | 1 | 18 |
| 2 | 20 | 40 | 4 | 80 |
| 3 | 12 | 36 | 9 | 108 |
| 4 | 9 | 36 | 16 | 144 |
| 5 | 7 | 35 | 25 | 175 |
| 6 | 3 | 18 | 36 | 108 |
| **Total**| **Σf = 75** | **Σ(fx) = 183** | | **Σ(fx²) = 633**|
2.  **Substitute totals into the formula:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{75 - 1} \left[ 633 - \frac{(183)^2}{75} \right]}$$
3.  **Calculate:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{74} \left[ 633 - \frac{33489}{75} \right]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{74} [633 - 446.52]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{74} [186.48]} \approx \sqrt{2.52} \approx 1.587$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures gives **1.59**.
:::

:::danger 28 May 2019, Section B, Question 24
30 employees had their punctuality monitored over a week to find out the number of times they were late for work. The results are shown below. Calculate the sample standard deviation of the data.

1, 4, 2, 1, 1, 1, 3, 3, 1, 3, 5, 1, 1, 2, 1, 2, 2, 2, 4, 3, 2, 3, 1, 1, 1, 1, 4, 1, 2, 2
:::
:::success Answer
Standard Deviation ≈ 1.24 ✔️
:::note Explanation

1.  **Create a frequency table from the raw data:**

| Number of times late (x) | Frequency (f) |
| :--- | :---: |
| 1 | 13 |
| 2 | 8 |
| 3 | 5 |
| 4 | 3 |
| 5 | 1 |
2.  **Create a working table:**

| Value (x) | Frequency (f) | f × x | x² | f × x² |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 1 | 13 | 13 | 1 | 13 |
| 2 | 8 | 16 | 4 | 32 |
| 3 | 5 | 15 | 9 | 45 |
| 4 | 3 | 12 | 16 | 48 |
| 5 | 1 | 5 | 25 | 25 |
| **Total**| **Σf = 30** | **Σ(fx) = 61** | | **Σ(fx²) = 163**|
3.  **Substitute totals into the formula:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{30 - 1} \left[ 163 - \frac{(61)^2}{30} \right]}$$
4.  **Calculate:**
$$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{29} \left[ 163 - \frac{3721}{30} \right]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{29} [163 - 124.033... ]}$$ $$s = \sqrt{\frac{1}{29} [38.966...]} \approx \sqrt{1.3436} \approx 1.159$$
:::
