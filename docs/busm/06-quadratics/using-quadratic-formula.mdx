### Solving Quadratic equation using Quadratic Formula

### What is the Quadratic Formula?

The quadratic formula is a universal tool used to find the solutions (also called **roots**) for any quadratic equation, which is an equation that can be written in the standard form:
$$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$$.

Its main advantage is that **it works every time**, even when the equation is difficult or impossible to solve by factoring.

---
### The Formula

The formula itself is:

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$$

Here, **a**, **b**, and **c** are the numerical coefficients from the standard form of the equation.

---
### Step-by-Step Guide to Using the Formula

Here is how to solve any quadratic equation using the formula. Let's use the example $2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0$.

#### **Step 1: Identify a, b, and c**

First, make sure your equation is in the standard form ($ax^2 + bx + c = 0$). Then, identify the values of a, b, and c.

* For $2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0$:
  * **a = 2** (the coefficient of $x^2$)
  * **b = 5** (the coefficient of $x$)
  * **c = -3** (the constant term)

#### **Step 2: Substitute the values into the formula**
Carefully replace a, b, and c in the formula with the values you identified. It's very important to pay attention to positive and negative signs.

$$x = \frac{-(5) \pm \sqrt{(5)^2 - 4(2)(-3)}}{2(2)}$$

#### **Step 3: Simplify the expression**

Solve the calculation step-by-step, following the order of operations (BODMAS). Start with the part inside the square root.

$$
x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 - (-24)}}{4}
$$

#### **Step 4: Calculate the two solutions**
The **±** symbol means you need to do the calculation twice: once using addition (+) and once using subtraction (-).

* **Solution 1 (with +):**
$$x = \frac{-5 + \sqrt{49}}{4} = \frac{-5 + 7}{4} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5$$

* **Solution 2 (with -):**
$$x = \frac{-5 - \sqrt{49}}{4} = \frac{-5 - 7}{4} = \frac{-12}{4} = -3$$

    So, the two solutions for the equation $2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0$ are **x = 0.5** and **x = -3**.

### Past Exam Questions
---

:::danger 19th November 2024, Section B, Question 23
Using the quadratic formula, find the exact solutions of: $2x^{2}-5x+1=0$. Then, give your answers to 2 d.p.
:::
:::success Answer
$x = 2.28$ and $x = 0.22$ ✔️
:::note Explanation
1.  **Identify a, b, c:** In $2x^2 - 5x + 1 = 0$, we have $a=2$, $b=-5$, and $c=1$.
2.  **Apply the Formula:** Substitute the values into the quadratic formula, $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$:
$$x = \frac{-(-5) \pm \sqrt{(-5)^2 - 4(2)(1)}}{2(2)}$$
3.  **Simplify:**
$$x = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 8}}{4} = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{17}}{4}$$
4.  **Calculate the two solutions:**
$$x \approx \frac{5 + 4.123}{4} \approx 2.28$$   $$x \approx \frac{5 - 4.123}{4} \approx 0.22$$
:::
